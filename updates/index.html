App release notes and changes.

Version 1 · Build 6

Apple Watch ranked races, heart rate, Live Activities, and push notifications

Apple Watch can now run fully standalone ranked races with a redesigned race screen and theming that matches the phone. Heart rate is captured on watch runs and shown across summaries, match history, and the feed. A new Lock Screen Live Activity tracks your ranked race against your ghost in real time. Real push notifications now alert you to likes, comments, replies, and friend requests even when the app is closed, and comments now support threaded replies. Phone runs sync to Apple Health, a new My Posts page lets you edit and delete shared runs, and smarter MMR helps misplaced players reach their true rank far faster — alongside GPS, pace, and feed map accuracy fixes.

Released June 25, 2026

Apple Watch Ranked Runs

  • The Apple Watch app can now run ranked races completely standalone, without your phone nearby. It races against a pool of cached real-player ghosts pushed from your phone, falling back to local bots when needed.
  • The watch home screen has been reworked into two clear buttons — Start Ranked and Start Normal — with a tortoise mark. Ranked stays disabled with a hint until you have completed your placement run.
  • Starting a ranked run now follows a proper flow: tap Ranked, a "finding opponent" search, a ready-up, a 10-second countdown, then the run. Phone-initiated matches share the same ready and countdown.
  • Bonus mode lets you keep running past the ranked 5 km. A finish banner shows your result while tracking continues, and your exact 5 km split is captured so bonus distance never corrupts your race time.
  • The watch theme now mirrors the iPhone app (cream monochrome or forest night) via a synced palette, and the watch app icon matches the phone icon.
  • Opening the watch app now pulls a fresh ghost pool, placement status, and theme from your phone rather than waiting on push timing.

Apple Watch Race Screen

  • Redesigned the ranked race screen. The two player progress bars have been removed in favour of a single hero number showing how far ahead or behind your ghost you are.
  • Pace is now larger, and the "remaining" readout has been replaced with your live heart rate.
  • The ahead indicator is now green to match the red behind indicator.
  • Redesigned the free-run watch screen too: a larger time and distance, plus a live heart-rate and pace row so heart rate, pace, distance, and time are all visible at once. The stats page now leads with those four metrics, with elevation as a slim footer.

Heart Rate

  • Apple Watch runs now capture average and max heart rate and sync them to your phone, where they are saved with the run.
  • Average and max heart rate now appear on the post-run summary, in match history detail, and on feed run detail. Tiles only show when heart-rate data exists, so phone-only runs without a sensor stay clean.
  • Live heart rate is now shown on the watch free-run and ranked race screens, with average and max styled in red on summaries to match.

Lock Screen Live Activity

  • Ranked races now show a Live Activity on your Lock Screen and in the Dynamic Island. It displays you versus your ghost opponent with dual progress bars to 5 km and your live lead and elapsed time.
  • The activity persists through bonus mode and ends with a WIN or LOSS and your finish time.

Push Notifications

  • Likes, comments, comment replies, and friend requests now arrive as real push notifications even when the app is closed, replacing the old in-app polling that only worked while the app was open.
  • A reply notifies the author of the comment you replied to, while a new top-level comment still notifies the post owner.

Comment Replies

  • You can now reply to a comment from the feed's comment sheet. Replies appear indented beneath the comment they answer.
  • A "Replying to @user" banner appears above the composer so it is clear who you are replying to.

Apple Health

  • Phone-recorded runs (both normal and ranked) now save to Apple Health as workouts, including distance, an estimated active-energy burn, and your GPS route.
  • Jeema asks for Health permission once after onboarding, and it is entirely optional.
  • Watch runs are intentionally excluded here because the watch already writes them to Health, avoiding duplicates.
  • Fixed the Apple Health badge on your profile always showing "Not Linked"; it now reflects your real connection status.

My Posts

  • A new My Posts page on your profile lets you view, edit (caption and photos), and delete runs you have shared to the feed.
  • My Posts and Run History cards now show a route preview map of each run.
  • Fixed multi-photo feed posts dropping photos when you shared before they had finished loading.

Social Feed

  • Fixed the feed route map appearing stretched and misaligned from the streets. Routes are now projected through MapKit just like match history, so they are correctly proportioned and aligned to the roads. This fix applies retroactively to every run already in the feed.
  • Runs shared from this build now upload their full-resolution route, so feed maps match the match-history detail exactly.
  • Feed and comment avatars now cache and retry transient network failures, fixing avatars getting stuck on the fallback initial after a flaky connection or while scrolling.
  • Feed post photos now use the same cached image loader, so they no longer fail to load on a flaky network or while scrolling and keep their spinner and fallback states.

Ranked & MMR

  • Ranked MMR now gently pulls toward the rating your actual 5 km pace implies, blended with the usual win/loss change. Misplaced players now reach their true rank in around 10–15 runs instead of 100 or more.
  • The pull self-zeroes as you converge on your true rating, and DNFs or interrupted runs earn no pace credit so they stay pure losses.
  • Fixed your fastest 5 km stat not updating from ranked runs. It now uses the exact 5 km crossing time rather than overall average pace, which was diluted when you continued past 5 km in bonus mode. The fix also applies to friend profiles.

Local Leaderboard

  • Fixed the local leaderboard reverting to your device region after you ran abroad. Jeema now caches your GPS-derived country and reuses it, instead of letting a location-less sync overwrite it with the device region.
  • On launch, the app reverse geocodes your most recent run's last GPS coordinate, so the local board follows where you actually ran even for runs recorded before this fix.

GPS & Pace Accuracy

  • Watch distance now uses HealthKit's fused GPS and pedometer distance instead of summing raw location deltas, with the old method kept as a fallback.
  • The phone now records every GPS fix and adds a stationary-jitter guard, so standing still no longer adds phantom distance.
  • Live pace on both phone and watch is now a smoothed ~20-second rolling average rather than an instantaneous or whole-run figure, so it reacts naturally without jumping around.

Privacy & Analytics

  • Restructured how Jeema handles analytics behind the scenes, with a cleaner internal setup and more reliable configuration in shipped builds.
Version 1 · Build 5

Photo sharing, social stats, and real GPS ranked distance

Ranked 5 km finish now triggers on real GPS distance only. Bot roster expanded to one bot every 50 MMR across all five tiers, so races should feel much closer. Attach up to 5 photos from your camera roll when sharing a run. Social feed now has a stats slide. Friend profiles now show WIN/LOSS on ranked runs and let you tap any run to see its route and stats. Local leaderboard now uses GPS location instead of device region setting.

Released June 15, 2026

Photo Sharing

  • You can now attach up to 5 photos from your camera roll when sharing a run to the feed.
  • Photos appear below the description field in the share composer on both the normal run and ranked run result screens.
  • A thumbnail strip previews selected photos before you post.
  • On the feed, swipe left on any post to browse the stats slide and then any attached photos. The map is always the first slide.

Social Feed

  • Swiping left on a feed post now shows a stats slide before the photos, displaying distance, pace, duration, elevation, and race duration for ranked runs.
  • The feed card always opens on the map slide when scrolling to a new post, even if you previously swiped to stats or photos on an earlier post.
  • Like and comment buttons have moved below the map card so they no longer overlap the route view.
  • The map and stats card now have a subtle black border for better definition.
  • Stat labels updated: DIST is now DISTANCE, RACE is now RACE TIME, and TOTAL is now TOTAL TIME.
  • Race and total times are now displayed in a cleaner minutes-and-seconds format (e.g. 36m 16s).
  • Elevation is now labelled in metres (m) rather than gain.
  • Removed the position indicator pip strip that appeared on the left side of the feed.

Friend Profiles

  • Ranked runs in a friend's recent runs list now show a WIN or LOSS badge in green or red alongside the opponent's name.
  • Tapping any run in a friend's recent runs list opens a detail sheet showing the route map and full run stats.
  • The run detail sheet now respects the app's monochrome or colour theme instead of always appearing dark.
  • The Add Friend button is no longer shown when viewing your own profile from the social feed.

Ranked Distance

  • Removed elevation compensation from the ranked finish trigger. The 5 km finish now fires at real GPS distance, matching how Strava and other apps measure your run. Previously, elevation gain was adding up to several hundred metres of credit, causing Jeema to record faster times than your actual GPS distance warranted.
  • Removed the elevation credit breakdown from match history rows and match detail sheets, as elevation no longer contributes to ranked distance.

Local Leaderboard

  • Fixed the local leaderboard incorrectly grouping all players under the same country. The app now reverse geocodes your GPS location at the end of a ranked run to determine your country, rather than reading the device's region setting. Players whose phone is set to a different region than where they actually run will now appear on the correct local leaderboard after their next ranked run.

Bot Improvements

  • Expanded the bot roster from 7 bots to one bot every 50 MMR, covering the full range from Tortoise through Falcon (MMR 50–14,000).
  • Bot paces are interpolated from known pace-to-MMR anchors, so each bot runs at the speed a real player at that rating would run.
  • The closest bot is now never more than 25 MMR away from any player, meaning races against bots should feel genuinely competitive rather than one-sided.
  • Each bot still has a small per-match pace variance so no two races feel identical.
Version 1 · Build 4

Race times, GPS fixes, and monochrome refresh

Ranked race times now show correctly across all screens. Match history shows elevation credit breakdowns. New info button explains MMR, tiers, and DNF rules. GPS false-start bug fixed. Run History and Friends screens refreshed to match the monochrome theme. Social feed polished. In-app friend request notifications added. Add-friend button now on profiles.

Released June 11, 2026

Run History

  • Refreshed the run history screen to better match Jeema's current monochrome theme.
  • Removed the large Run History title from the sheet.
  • Updated the Done button to match the compact style used across other profile sheets.
  • Added stronger black accents to each run card so distance, time, pace, and run type are easier to scan.
  • Kept the page background light so the screen feels consistent with the rest of the app.

Friends

  • Refreshed the Friends tab with a cleaner monochrome look.
  • Replaced the old dark green styling with light grey backgrounds, white cards, black buttons, and clearer row borders.
  • Search results, pending requests, and friend rows now use a more consistent card layout.
  • Friend avatars and action buttons now better match the current Jeema theme.

Friend Requests

  • Jeema can now show a notification when a new friend request is detected while the app is running.
  • Friend request notifications use the sender's username so it is clear who wants to connect.
  • Foreground notifications can now show as banners instead of being silently hidden while the app is open.

Ranked Match History

  • Match history now shows when elevation has added ranked distance credit.
  • Match details now include a clearer breakdown of GPS distance, elevation credit, and ranked effort distance.
  • This should make hilly ranked runs easier to understand when the GPS distance appears slightly under 5 km.
  • Fixed a race condition where match history could reload before the ranked result had finished writing to the server, causing the latest run to appear missing until the next app launch.

Ranked Data Reliability

  • Fixed a bug where wins and losses could revert to a stale count after a ranked run if the result failed to write to the server. Wins and losses now always keep the highest value seen, so a network hiccup can no longer undo a recorded result.
  • Fixed app launch sync to use the higher of two data sources when reconciling wins and losses, so a missed write on one path cannot overwrite a correct count from the other.
  • Ranked results that fail to upload due to poor signal are now saved locally and retried automatically the next time a connection is available. No ranked run result should be permanently lost due to a bad connection.

Ranked System Info

  • Added an info button to the top-right corner of the ranked card. Tapping it opens a sheet explaining what MMR is, how the 5 km ghost race format works, how MMR changes are calculated, and the full tier structure from Tortoise to Falcon.
  • The ranked info sheet now includes a "FINISHING & DNFs" section explaining that you must physically complete the full 5 km to finish a race, that stopping before 5 km is a DNF and counts as a loss, and that continuing to run after 5 km still tracks all stats.

Ranked Race Times

  • The bonus running screen now shows your locked 5 km race time on the result card while you continue running.
  • The end-run result screen now shows a RACE TIME / TOTAL RUN split when you ran past 5 km, so your finish time is never confused with your total run time.
  • The social feed now shows RACE TIME for ranked posts. If the runner continued past 5 km, both RACE and TOTAL times are shown.
  • Match history rows now display the locked 5 km finish time instead of the total run duration.
  • Match history detail view now shows RACE TIME (and TOTAL TIME when applicable). Pace is now calculated from the 5 km race time rather than the full run duration.

Social Feed

  • Like and comment buttons on the social feed now use white for better contrast against the dark reel background.
  • Liking a post now triggers haptic feedback. The same feedback fires when double-tapping to like.
  • Fixed an issue where some users' avatars were not showing on the social feed even though the user had a profile photo set. Avatar and username lookups are now handled by separate focused queries, which avoids a PostgREST behaviour where fetching both columns together could silently drop the avatar for some rows.

Member Duration

  • Fixed the "MEMBER FOR X DAYS" counter showing one day short. A user who joined today now correctly shows "MEMBER FOR 1 DAY".

Friend Profiles

  • An "ADD FRIEND" button now appears on a user's profile when you are not yet friends. Tapping it sends a friend request.
  • If a request is already pending, the button changes to "REQUEST SENT · TAP TO CANCEL" so you can withdraw it.
  • If the other person has already sent you a request, a banner makes that clear.

GPS Distance Accuracy

  • Fixed a bug where runs could show an extra 30–50 metres of false distance at the start. iOS delivers stale cached location fixes immediately when tracking begins, and these were being counted as real movement. The app now discards any fix whose timestamp predates the moment tracking started.
  • The same stale-fix protection now applies after unpausing a run, preventing a burst of false distance from a cached location being measured against the pre-pause position.
  • Tightened the GPS accuracy filter so only fixes with less than 25 metres of horizontal uncertainty are used for distance calculations (previously up to 50 metres was accepted). This reduces the path zigzag that caused Jeema to read ahead of other apps like Strava over longer runs.
Version 1 · Build 3

Ranked runs, leaderboards, profiles, and post-run polish

This update focuses on ranked runs, leaderboards, player profiles, and post-run polish.

Released June 5, 2026

Permissions

  • Location and notification permissions are now handled during first-launch setup.
  • Run screens no longer trigger surprise permission popups when starting a run.
  • If location permission is not fully enabled, the setup screen now lets users retry or open Settings.
  • If iOS does not show the Always Allow popup, Jeema now gives clearer instructions to enable it from Settings.
  • Notifications remain optional, but Always Allow location is required for reliable run tracking.

Ranked Runs

  • Players now appear on the leaderboard after completing their first placement run.
  • Your first placement run now counts toward your running stats.
  • Ranked runs now save to your stats as soon as the 5 km ranked result is completed.
  • If you continue running after the ranked 5 km, the extra distance still updates the saved run.
  • Apple Watch ranked completions now count toward stats even when only the ranked finish time is received.

Post-Run Results

  • The ranked post-5 km screen has been simplified.
  • Result cards now show a clear VICTORY or DEFEAT state instead of comparing your time against the opponent's time.
  • Win and loss cards now use softer theme-friendly green and red colors.
  • Defeat text and supporting text are easier to read.
  • The post-run copy now uses Jeema branding.
  • The share-to-feed ranked result screen no longer uses the old gold theme.

Leaderboards

  • Leaderboard visibility now starts after one placement run.
  • Global, regional, friends, and division leaderboards all use the updated placement requirement.
  • Tapping players from the leaderboard now opens their public profile view, not just friend profiles.

Player Profiles

  • Player profiles now include a fuller stats view.
  • Profile stats now show mileage, season/all-time stats, elevation, time run, streaks, and personal bests.
  • The mileage card on player profiles now matches the stats page design.
  • Public player profiles no longer show private or editable goal controls.

Previews And Development Fixes

  • Fixed ranked run previews that were failing because they depended on live location tracking.
  • Fixed run history previews that were crashing from missing preview environment data.
  • Added a preview for the ranked share-to-feed result screen.

Visual Cleanup

  • Removed more of the old gold styling from ranked result screens.
  • Updated ranked result colors to better match the current app theme.
